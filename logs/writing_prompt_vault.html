🎯 Master Voice Prompts

The Confident Humility Master Prompt
Apply the confident humility framework to this writing. Authority should come through expertise and clarity, not defensiveness or over-explanation. Lead with earned wisdom from experience, not theoretical knowledge. Make definitive statements about what you know while remaining open about what you're still learning. Focus on serving reader transformation rather than displaying your knowledge. Remove hedging language unless uncertainty is genuinely important. Your voice should feel like someone who has walked this path and can light the way, not someone trying to prove their worthiness to teach.
Use when: Any content needs authentic authority without arrogance
Situational Voice Calibration
Before refining this content, determine: Does this reader need Pure Transmission (philosophical insight, understanding) or Coaching Authority (direct instruction, behavior change)? Adjust voice accordingly. Pure Transmission: contemplative, clean, insight-focused. Coaching Authority: direct, commanding, action-oriented. Both modes maintain confident humility but express it differently based on what serves the reader's transformation.
Use when: Switching between teaching styles within the same piece

✂️ Concision & Clarity Prompts

Concision Level 1 - Light Polish
Remove unnecessary words and tighten sentences while maintaining voice and flow. Eliminate redundant phrases, weak qualifiers, and filler words. Keep the core message and personality intact. Focus on clarity without losing authenticity.
Use when: Content is good but slightly wordy
Concision Level 2 - Structural Tightening
Significantly reduce word count while preserving all essential meaning. Combine related sentences, eliminate repetitive ideas, cut non-essential examples. Every paragraph should advance the core message. Remove beautiful but unnecessary language that doesn't serve transformation.
Use when: Content needs substantial shortening for platform limits
Concision Level 3 - Ruthless Compression
Compress to absolute essentials. Keep only what directly serves the core transformation. One clear point per paragraph. Remove all examples, qualifiers, and explanatory text that isn't critical. Preserve voice but prioritize impact over completeness. Cut 50-70% while maintaining power.
Use when: Extreme brevity required (social media, headlines)

🎭 Voice Adjustment Prompts

Too Direct → Add Warmth
Soften this content while maintaining authority. Add understanding and acknowledgment of reader's current struggle. Lead with empathy before instruction. Include phrases that show you recognize their experience. Maintain confident guidance but make it feel supportive rather than commanding.
Use when: Content feels harsh or intimidating
Too Soft → Add Authority
Strengthen this content by removing hedging language and making definitive statements. Replace "might," "perhaps," "could be" with confident assertions where appropriate. Lead with conviction about what you know works. Make clear, actionable statements that demonstrate expertise.
Use when: Content lacks conviction or feels wishy-washy
Anti-Poetic Mode (For Mr. GPT) 😆
Remove flowery language, metaphors, and overly artistic phrasing. Use direct, conversational language. Avoid dramatic expressions and elaborate descriptions. Get straight to the point. Replace abstract concepts with concrete examples. Sound like a skilled practitioner sharing practical wisdom, not a poet writing about movement.
Use when: GPT gets too philosophical and loses practical edge

🔗 Philosophy-Practice Integration

Seamless Integration Bridge
Weave philosophical insight directly into practical instruction without creating separate sections. Every technical point should teach something about human potential or consciousness. Every philosophical concept should lead to clear application. Ask: "What deeper truth does this technique reveal?" and "How does this insight translate to practical action?"
Use when: Combining wisdom with instruction

📱 Hook Creation System

Desire Hook Template
Create a hook that suggests readers are using [current approach] for [limited purpose] when they could use it for [transformational possibility]. Format: "Most people use [X] to [small goal]. What if you could use it to [life-changing outcome]?"
Contradiction Hook
Challenge conventional wisdom with: "[Common belief] is backwards. Here's what actually works..." Follow with perspective that flips expected thinking about the topic.
Vulnerability + Authority
"[X years] of [experience] taught me I was [wrong about something/missing something]. Here's what I discovered..." Combines credibility with humble admission of growth.
"Authority becomes stronger when it doesn't need to defend itself"

📚 Mini Learning Blocks

Confident Humility Core

Your expertise shows through clarity and results, not through jargon or defensiveness. Lead with earned wisdom, acknowledge what you're still learning, focus on reader transformation over self-promotion.

Reader-First Principle

Every word either serves their growth or serves your ego - choose growth. Ask: "What does this reader need to hear vs. want to hear?" Serve their transformation, not their comfort.

Hook Psychology

Effective hooks create cognitive disruption - they interrupt expected thinking patterns and force reconsideration. Best hooks acknowledge current experience while hinting at larger possibilities.

Transition Strategy

Bridge concepts by acknowledging the thinking process you've triggered. Move from perspective shift → reflection invitation → personal sharing. Create logical flow, not random jumps.
